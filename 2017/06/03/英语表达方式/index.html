英文表达方式 - 第一篇

发表于 2017-06-03 | | 阅读次数

从读研到工作期间，我的大部分的英语都是和图片中的这个哥所学，他是居住在宾夕法尼亚洲的一个美国人。研究生毕业时的论文英文摘要在盲审的时候老师也表扬了我，说，你的英文不错嘛？ 呵呵呵，诸不知，我论文的英语摘要是这个哥帮我所写。回报是：我教他中文。此外，还有技术上的一些交流，目前他在 Penn State 读数据分析专业。爱好：打游戏，喝酒，抽烟，做炸药，anything. 我在想，还是把很多交流记录下来服务大家, 因为知识是需要共享的。

  • Why don’t you look up the definition of upon ?

  • A common phrase is “Upon closer inspection”, which is like saying “After closer inspection”.

  • Upon serialization the names of these fields are serialized = After serialization, the name of ….

  • What odd about that ? Odd is an adjective which means strange or unexpected.

  • Drinking a cold beer is so refreshing. Refreshing is an adjective in this context.

  • Do you think it’s weird too ? Oh, so just having is weird.

  • Having to deal with you is a pain in the ass John.

  • Having discharged my duty, I bid you farewell.

    Discharged 在这里的意思是 to perform a task, expecially an official one. Bid 在这里的意思是 to give a greeting to someone, or to ask someone to do someting. means 打招呼, 致歉以及请求. E.g. I must now bid you farewell which means 我现在必须向您道别了。

  • Having a calculator do all the work for you is too easy.

  • Having too many books is never a bad thing.

  • Having an apple makes me happy.
    Having in this case is a present participle, and participle clauses can get a little tricky. clause in this context means a group of words, consisting of a subject and a finite form of a verb. And tricky is an adjective in this case which means if a piece of work or problem is tricky, it is difficult to deal with and needs careful attention or skills.

  • Having won the match, Susan jumped for joy.

  • Having been told the bad news, she sat down and cried.
    It emphasizes that one action was before another. Or you could also reverse it like Susan jumped for joy, having won the match.

Okay, let me make a sentence using “having”.

  • Me: Having fixed the bug. Correct ?

  • Garian: Having fixed the bug… ? What comes next ? When you use having, I expect you to follow it up with another clause, otherwise it’s invalid. Like, Having fixed the bug, the program was able to function properly.

  • It is just instinct for me, I know when things are correct or incorrect, but I have no idea why. When you use having in this way, it should be followed with only one past tense verb and that is the pattern.

还有一种情况是，你的英文看上去并没有什么问题，可是和人交流的时候就像是书本上的话，这样是很不好的。比如下面的：

  • After her having broken up with me, I cried. And he thinks that’s technically correct, but it sounds really awful doesn’t it ? Because it’s too many words and sounds bloated, and stuffy, and flowery. When you could just say, I cried after she broke up with me and that’s clear, succinct, and directly to the point. Good writing.

还有 I would say is better than I say. People usually just say, i’d say because it sounds better. I dont even think “I say” is correct. It is just a dialect thing.
